# AldHome
Game for Idaho University game jam 2026

Activity description: what were the expectations and what did you do?
For the 2026 Vandal game jam, we were asked to create a game with the theme of "egg" and the limitation of "you control the enviroment".

Technical decisions: what decisions did you take from a technical perspective throughout this activity/event?
I decided to do a simple terminal text-based game due to couple factors, mainly the lack of time, and the fact that I was working alone. I used c++ because it was the language I am the most comfortable with.

Contributions: did you participate alone or with a team? If with a team, what were your specific individual contributions?
I worked alone.

Quality assessment: how would you assess your participation in this event? What would you do differently if you could redo the event?
I believe that if I had more time, I could have done more, however I had multiple other things that required my attention. In the past I was able to do game jam competitions with minimal homework. For this jam I was actually busy a large portion of Saturday with homework, and did a team meeting for the 360 project on Sunday. While I would have likely stuck with a text-based format, I believe that without extraneous responsibilities, I would have been able to submit a much more in-depth and polished game.
